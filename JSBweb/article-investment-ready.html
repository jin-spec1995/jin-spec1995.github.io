Is Your Business Ready for Investment? A 7-Point Checklist

By Keith Davidson | Published October 15, 2025

Securing investment can be a game-changer for a growing business, but it's about much more than just having a great idea. Investors are looking for well-managed, scalable businesses that have a clear plan for generating a return. Before you even think about creating a pitch deck, you need to get your house in order.

Our InvestReady Program guides businesses through this entire process. Here is a 7-point checklist to assess if you're on the right track.

1. Do You Have a Clear, Scalable Business Model?

Investors need to see how their money will help you grow exponentially, not just incrementally. A scalable model means you can increase revenue without a proportional increase in costs. Can you articulate exactly how your business makes money and how that will scale?

2. Are Your Financials in Order?

This is non-negotiable. You need clean, accurate, and up-to-date financial records, including a Profit & Loss statement, Balance Sheet, and a Cash Flow Statement. More importantly, you need a detailed financial forecast for the next 3-5 years showing how you plan to use the investment and the expected return.

3. Do You Understand Your Market and Competitors?

Investors will test your knowledge here. You need to demonstrate a deep understanding of your target market size, your ideal customer, and your competitive landscape. What makes you different and why can you win?

4. Is There a Strong Management Team in Place?

Often, investors bet on the team as much as the idea. Does your team have the right mix of skills and experience to execute the plan? Have you identified any key gaps in your team that the investment could help fill?

5. Do You Have Proof of Concept or Traction?

An idea is one thing; a business is another. Have you demonstrated that customers actually want what you're selling? Traction could be early sales, a growing user base, letters of intent, or successful pilot programs. The more traction you have, the less risk for the investor.

6. Is Your Legal Structure Clean?

Investors need to know they are investing in a clean, properly structured company. Ensure your business is registered correctly (typically as a Proprietary Limited company), that you have a clear shareholders' agreement if you have co-founders, and that you own all the necessary intellectual property (IP).

7. Do You Know Exactly How Much You Need and What You'll Use It For?

A vague request for money is a major red flag. You must have a specific "ask" (e.g., "$250,000") and a detailed breakdown of how you will spend it (e.g., "$100k for key hires, $80k for marketing, $70k for product development"). This shows you are a serious and disciplined operator.

Getting "investor-ready" is a rigorous process, but it's one that builds a stronger, more resilient business regardless of whether you secure funding. If you can confidently tick these seven boxes, you're in a great position to start the conversation.
